ABOUT

About

I am a mathematician. My doctoral research, under Sir Simon Donaldson at Imperial College London, was in gauge theory on G₂-manifolds. Before that, a Diploma in Physics from the University of Turin, with a thesis in theoretical particle physics.

I have taught at university level in Berlin for over twenty years — 66 different courses across HTW Berlin, Berliner Hochschule für Technik, and TU Berlin, spanning pure mathematics, applied mathematics, physics, and computer science — and at the Nesin Mathematics Village in Turkey since 2019. Alongside this, I have tutored privately for thirty years: roughly a thousand students, from school exams to graduate coursework, with eighty reviews on Superprof, all five stars.

Most students come to me stuck — behind in a course, weeks from an exam, or simply not getting what they need from a lecture of two hundred people. I work one-to-one, at the pace the material actually requires, until it makes sense. Many stay for a single exam; others work with me for years, through an entire degree, including bachelor's and master's thesis supervision.

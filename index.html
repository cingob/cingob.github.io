ABOUT

About

I am a mathematician. My doctoral research, under Sir Simon Donaldson at Imperial College London, was in gauge theory on G₂-manifolds — a domain at the intersection of differential topology, differential geometry, and mathematical physics. Before that, a Diploma in Physics from the University of Turin, with a thesis in theoretical particle physics.

I have held more than eighty university courses across HTW Berlin, Berliner Hochschule für Technik, and TU Berlin, spanning the full range of pure and applied mathematics, physics, and computer science. Since 2019 I teach annually at the Nesin Mathematics Village in Turkey, a residential school for advanced mathematics.

What I offer privately is not remedial instruction but sustained intellectual engagement: the kind of teaching that changes how a student thinks — about proof, about abstraction, about the connections between fields — not just whether they pass the next examination. Many students work with me for the duration of a degree. Others come for a single decisive preparation. I have supervised bachelor's and master's theses in subjects from algebraic geometry to stochastic calculus.
